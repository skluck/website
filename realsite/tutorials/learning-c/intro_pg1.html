Page 1: Introduction

Why C?

As one of the world's first high-level languages, C plays a vital role in virtually every operating system.
Thanks to its combined high-level syntax and low-level facilities, C remains one of the world's most popular languages,
enjoying usage in every area of programming.
C is especially important in systems programming and hardware interfacing, making it a must-know for aspiring systems developers.
This tutorials will introduce you to the syntax of C (C89 and C99), and will allow you to approach and solve your own problems through C.

Who is this guide for?

This guide is primarily intended for programmers and developers with experience in other languages.
A background in ALGOL-like languages (Java, C++, Perl, etc) may make it easier to understand C's syntax and follow this guide.
If you don't have any prior experience with programming, you'll probably find it easier to read a book on the subject.

What is C for?

Although C is incredibly powerful, it's not for everything.
Its relatively small standard library and memory footprint make it ideal for high-performance computing, but also force the programmer to "reinvent the wheel."
When development time matters more than execution time, other languages like Java, Perl, and bash should be considered for their more expansive facilities.

Requirements

To follow this guide, you're going to need several programs installed on your computer:

  • A C compiler like gcc, clang, tcc, or msvc.
  • A text editor (or IDE) like vi(m), emacs, Sublime Text, or Notepad(++). Syntax highlighting is a major plus.
  • Recommended: A Unix or Unix-like operating system like OS X, Linux, or BSD. On Windows, Cygwin/MinGW is strongly recommended.
